# bl.okk

A sliding block puzzle in the spirit of *Unblock Me*, built from the same five
realistic wooden blocks, tray environment and rendering stack as
[kl.oss.ete](https://klossete.iverfinne.no). Slide the planks aside and steer
the **red cylinder** out through the gap in the tray wall.

Built with **Next.js 16**, **React Three Fiber** and **drei**.

## The pieces

Every piece keeps its real millimetre size; one grid cell is 15 mm, so each
footprint lands exactly on whole cells.

| Piece | Size | Cells | Movement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dark Blue Plank | 30 × 75 × 15 mm | 2 × 5 | along its length only |
| Dark Blue Short | 30 × 60 × 15 mm | 2 × 4 | along its length only |
| Light Blue Cube | 30 × 30 × 30 mm | 2 × 2 | both axes |
| Orange Block | 45 × 45 × 24 mm | 3 × 3 | both axes |
| Red Cylinder | Ø 30 mm · H 60 mm | 2 × 2 | both axes — the hero |

## Levels

The 23 levels are generated by `scripts/generate-levels.mjs`, which scatters
pieces on the board and BFS-solves each candidate, keeping only layouts whose
*minimum* solution lands in the target difficulty band, then sorted by
verified difficulty (`scripts/sort-levels.mjs`) into one smooth ramp from 4
to 28 minimum moves. Exit position, board size and piece mix vary as the set
progresses.

The scene is a pure white studio: an elevated slab fills the view, the board
is a pocket recessed into it, and the exit is a floor-level channel cut
through the slab — only cast shadows and ambient occlusion draw the geometry.

There is no UI at all — no text, no buttons. The channel says where to go,
solving a level rolls straight into the next (wrapping after the last), and
progress is saved locally. Undo is sliding a piece back. On desktop,
invisible shortcuts exist: `Z` undo, `R` restart, digits `1–9` jump between
levels.

- `pnpm levels` – regenerate the level set
- `pnpm solve` – re-validate `lib/levels.json` against the solver

## Getting started

```bash
pnpm install
pnpm dev
```

Then open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000).

## Project structure

- `components/sliding-blocks.tsx` – the 3D scene, sliding/clamping logic, HUD
- `components/engine/PostFx.tsx` – klossete's post-processing stack
- `lib/blocks.ts` – the block catalogue mapped onto the 15 mm grid
- `lib/levels.json` – the generated, solver-verified puzzle set
- `lib/impact-sound.ts` – synthesised wooden knock sounds (Web Audio)
- `scripts/` – level generator + validator
